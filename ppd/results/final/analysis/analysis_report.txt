## 2. KEY FINDINGS
------------------------------------------------------------

### 2.1 PPD vs PD (KV Transfer Avoidance)
  - PPD beats PD in T2 TTFT: 108 scenarios
  - Reason: PPD uses prefix cache for Turn 2+, avoiding KV transfer overhead
  - Typical improvement: 30-70% faster T2 TTFT

### 2.2 PD TPOT Advantage in Big Paste (_d) Workloads
  - PD wins TPOT in _d workloads: 19 scenarios
  - Examples: L_d@QPS=3, L_d@QPS=4, M_d@QPS=2, M_d@QPS=3, M_d@QPS=4...
  - Reason: _d has large T2 prefill (512 tokens) + small decode (64 tokens)
  - PD isolates large prefill on P-machines, D-machines only do decode
  - PPD/Replica: D-machine does both prefill+decode -> resource contention
  - KEY INSIGHT: When prefill is heavy, PD isolation benefits TPOT!

### 2.3 Replica Advantage at High QPS
  - Replica wins at high QPS (>=3): 16 scenarios
  - Reason: 4 independent workers provide double capacity
  - Trade-off: No prefill-decode isolation, potential HOL blocking

### 2.4 Context Size Impact
  - Small (256+256): Replica (Data Parallel) wins most (13/24)
  - Medium (512+512): PPD (Prefix-aware) wins most (19/24)
  - Large (1024+1024): PPD (Prefix-aware) wins most (13/16)
  - XLarge (2048+1024): PPD (Prefix-aware) wins most (8/16)

### 2.5 T2 Type Impact
  - Tiny (16→32): Replica (Data Parallel) wins most (14/27)
  - Short Q Long Out (32→256): PPD (Prefix-aware) wins most (15/27)
  - Balanced (128→128): PPD (Prefix-aware) wins most (20/27)
  - Big Paste (512→64): PPD (Prefix-aware) wins most (23/27)

## 3. RECOMMENDATIONS BY OPTIMIZATION GOAL
------------------------------------------------------------

### 3.1 Optimize for TTFT (User Responsiveness)
  1. LOW QPS (<2): Use PPD - prefix cache benefit dominates
  2. MEDIUM QPS (2-4): Use PPD for most workloads
  3. HIGH QPS (>4): Consider Replica for small contexts
  4. TINY T2 (type 'a'): Replica competitive due to capacity
  5. OTHER T2 types: PPD strongly preferred

### 3.2 Optimize for TPOT (Generation Quality)
  1. BIG PASTE (_d): Use PD - isolation prevents decode interference
  2. HIGH QPS + _d: PD has significant advantage (up to 50% better TPOT)
  3. OTHER workloads: PPD/Replica similar, Replica slightly better at high QPS
  4. KEY: If T2 prefill is heavy, PD isolation benefits decode quality!

### 3.3 Summary Decision Matrix
  | Scenario              | TTFT Target | TPOT Target |
  |-----------------------|-------------|-------------|
  | Low QPS, any workload | PPD         | PPD         |
  | High QPS, tiny T2     | Replica     | Replica     |
  | High QPS, big paste   | PPD         | PD          |
  | High QPS, other       | PPD/Replica | Replica     |
